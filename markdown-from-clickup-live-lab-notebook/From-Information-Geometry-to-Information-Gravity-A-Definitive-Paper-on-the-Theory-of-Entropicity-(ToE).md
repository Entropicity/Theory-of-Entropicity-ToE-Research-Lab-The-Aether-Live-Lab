# From Information Geometry to Information Gravity: A Definitive Paper on the Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

The definitive paper titled **"From Information Geometry to Information Gravity (Information Geometry as the Origin of Einstein's Gravity)"** was written by theoretical researcher, physicist, and philosopher **John Onimisi Obidi.** [1, 2] 

## Key Aspects of the Paper
Published as part of the Theory of Entropicity (ToE) series, the paper bridges statistical physics and general relativity by introducing several core frameworks: [3, 4, 5] 

* The Obidi Action: A mathematical variational principle that acts upon an information-entropic field, mapping it to a physical, dynamical arena just as the Einstein–Hilbert action does for spacetime. [6] 
* Emergent Relativity: The framework recovers Einstein’s classical field equations as a low-gradient, near-equilibrium limit of entropic dynamics, rather than treating gravity as a fundamental geometric postulate. [6] 
* The Alpha-Q Constraint: It mathematically links Rényi-Tsallis functional deformations to affine geometric asymmetry, explaining how the curvature of spacetime is fundamentally derived from information density. [6] 

## Broad Context & Influences
Obidi's work synthesizes and builds heavily upon previous seminal foundations in entropic gravity:

   1. **Ariel Caticha (2019):** Modeled a "blurred space" using maximum entropy methods, deriving vacuum gravity directly from the geometry of inference. [7] 
   2. **Erik Verlinde & Ted Jacobson:** Highly influential physicists who pioneered the thermodynamic and entropic interpretations of gravity, which Obidi reviews and expands upon. [6, 8, 9, 10, 11]
