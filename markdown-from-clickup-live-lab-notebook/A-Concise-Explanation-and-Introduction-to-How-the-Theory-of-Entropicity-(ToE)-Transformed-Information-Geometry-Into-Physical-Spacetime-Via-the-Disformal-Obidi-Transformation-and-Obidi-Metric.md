# Introduction

The **Theory of Entropicity (ToE)**, proposed by **John Onimisi Obidi**, presents a radical reinterpretation of fundamental physics, elevating entropy from a mere statistical measure of disorder to the foundational field of existence. This framework aims to unify disparate areas of physics, including general relativity, quantum mechanics, and information theory, by positing that space, time, motion, and gravity all emerge from the dynamics of this fundamental entropic field [1] [2] [3].

# Core Premise of the Theory of Entropicity

Unlike conventional physics, which often treats entropy as a secondary, emergent property, ToE asserts that entropy is an ontological scalar field that permeates all of existence. All physical laws, geometry, and interactions are proposed to arise naturally from the flow and curvature of this entropic field. In this worldview, the geometry of space, the flow of time, and the dynamics of motion are all manifestations of the entropic field [2] [4].

# Contrast with Einstein's General Relativity

Einstein's General Relativity posits that spacetime is a fundamental, geometric entity, and matter/energy curves this spacetime, which in turn dictates how matter moves (the **Einstein-Wheeler** dictum: **and"matter tells spacetime how to curve, and spacetime tells matter how to move**"). ToE, however, flips this script. It suggests that spacetime itself is an emergent manifestation of the entropic field's dynamic structure. Gravity, in this view, is not a fundamental force but arises as the curvature response of the entropic field, with matter being localized regions of entropic condensation [2] [4].

# Information Geometry: The Pre-Spacetime Structure

Information geometry is a mathematical framework that applies geometric concepts to probability distributions. It describes a manifold where points represent probability distributions, and distances measure how distinguishable these distributions are. 

# Key components of information geometry include:

•**Fisher-Rao metric:** A natural metric on the space of probability distributions, quantifying statistical distinguishability [1] [2].

•**α-connections:** A family of affine connections that describe how information changes when probability models are updated [1] [2].

•**Curvature:** Statistical curvature arising from the geometry of probability distributions [1].

However, information geometry, by itself, is static. It possesses a metric, connections, and curvature, but it lacks dynamics, evolution, field equations, or inherent physical meaning. It provides a geometric language without a physical story [1].

# The Obidi Action: Imparting Dynamics to Information Geometry

The crucial innovation of ToE lies in the introduction of the Obidi Action. This variational principle is central to ToE, encoding the dynamics of the entropy field. Analogous to the Einstein-Hilbert action in General Relativity, which provides dynamics to the spacetime metric, the Obidi Action provides dynamics to information geometry [1] [2] [4].

The Obidi Action is a functional, denoted as O[gij,S,∇,…], built from the Fisher-Rao metric, entropic curvature, α-connections, entropic fields, and information potentials. Crucially, this action is defined on the information manifold, not on spacetime [1].
