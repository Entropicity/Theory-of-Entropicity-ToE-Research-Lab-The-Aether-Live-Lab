John Onimisi Obidi’s "Theory of Entropicity" (ToE) is an audacious theoretical framework that recasts entropy not as a byproduct of disorder, but as the fundamental physical field and foundational substrate of the universe. It attempts to unify quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, and gravity from first principles.The Core PillarsOntological Primacy of Entropy: Obidi’s first conjecture proposes that everything we experience—spacetime, mass, energy, and information—is simply an emergent, macro-shadow of an underlying, dynamic entropic field.The Obidi Action & MEE: The mathematical core of the theory relies on a variational principle called the Obidi Action. From this, the Master Entropic Equation (MEE) is derived, functioning as an entropic analogue to Einstein's Field Equations in General Relativity.The "No-Rush" Theorem: Rather than treating the speed of light (c) as an arbitrary geometric constant, ToE defines it as the maximum throughput rate at which the entropic field can reorganize its information.Philosophical & Conceptual ImplicationsOntodynamics: Obidi establishes a new philosophical discipline termed "Ontodynamics"—the study of existence itself as stabilized entropic motion. In this view, "Being" (existence) is the persistence of entropic gradients, while "Becoming" (change) is irreversible entropic redistribution.Decoupling from the Observer: Unlike some interpretations of quantum mechanics that center privileged observers, ToE frames consciousness as an emergent phenomenon—defined as systems running specialized, internal entropy-processing loops.Bridging Frameworks: Obidi's research serves as a meta-framework that encompasses other entropic and emergent gravity concepts, notably aligning with the research of Erik Verlinde, Ted Jacobson, and Ginestra Bianconi.To explore the exact mathematical formulations and conceptual breakdown of the theory, you can review the author's open-access papers housed on the Open Science Framework or read the technical breakdowns on Medium.Would you like to explore how the Theory of Entropicity resolves specific physical problems like quantum wave-function collapse or dark matter, or dive deeper into the philosophical foundations of Ontodynamics?
