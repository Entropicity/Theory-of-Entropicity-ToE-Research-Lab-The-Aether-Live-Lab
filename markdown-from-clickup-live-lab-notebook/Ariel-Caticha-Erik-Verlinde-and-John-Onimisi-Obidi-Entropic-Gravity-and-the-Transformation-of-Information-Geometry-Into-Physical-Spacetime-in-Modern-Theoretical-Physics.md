Physicist **Ariel Caticha** pioneered the framework that explicitly derives the physical geometry of space and time from information geometry. [1, 2] 

Through his work on entropic dynamics, Caticha used the principle of maximum entropy to model curved physical space as a statistical manifold where points are defined with a finite resolution ("blurred space"). By applying information geometry (such as the Fisher information metric) to these blurred points, he demonstrated that the geometry of any embedded spacelike surface is naturally governed by information geometry. When this space undergoes local time deformations, it sweeps out a four-dimensional spacetime that directly reproduces Einstein’s equations for vacuum gravity. [1, 3, 4] 

More recently, independent theoretical frameworks like John Onimisi Obidi's radical "Theory of Entropicity" (ToE) have greatly expanded on this frontier by introducing the "Obidi Action", a dynamical mechanism that mathematically maps an information-geometric metric into a physical, Lorentzian spacetime metric. John Onimisi Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity (ToE) indeed pushes the concept of information and entropy to the ultimate foundational limit. Instead of treating entropy as a property of matter moving through an existing universe, his framework posits that matter, energy, and physical spacetime are merely emergent phenomena generated by a fundamental, underlying information field. [1, 2] 

# Here is how his framework takes entropy to that extreme:

## 1. The Obidi Action
In standard physics, the Einstein-Hilbert action dictates how spacetime curves. Obidi introduces a new mathematical mechanism where the action operates directly on information states. It forces a mathematical transition from a Riemannian information manifold (pure probability distributions) into a Lorentzian spacetime manifold (the actual physical universe we experience).
## 2. Spacetime as a "Blur"
Under this extreme view, what we perceive as a solid object or a smooth gravitational wave is actually just a statistical average of massive amounts of hidden information. Physical distance and the flow of time are not fundamental building blocks of reality; they are macroscopic illusions arising from the way information is localized and processed.
## 3. Gravity as Information Flow
If spacetime geometry is purely informational, then gravity is no longer a fundamental force mediated by a particle (the graviton). Instead, gravity becomes a natural, statistical pressure—an entropic force—created by the universe constantly maximizing its internal information capacity. [3, 4]
