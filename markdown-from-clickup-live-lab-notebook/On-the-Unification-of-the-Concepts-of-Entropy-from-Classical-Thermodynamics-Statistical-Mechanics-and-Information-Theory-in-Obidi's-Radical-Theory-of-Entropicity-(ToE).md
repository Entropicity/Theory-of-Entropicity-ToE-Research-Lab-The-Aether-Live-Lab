On the Unification of the Concepts of Entropy from Classical Thermodynamics, Statistical Mechanics, and Information Theory in Obidi's Radical Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

In John Onimisi Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity (ToE), the three classical frameworks of entropy are unified and elevated from passive measurement tools into a single, fundamental physical field ($S(x)$) that acts as the primary substrate of reality. [1, 2, 3] 
Instead of treating thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, and information theory as separate perspectives, Obidi’s Ontodynamics synthesizes them. It treats the universe as a continuous entropic computation where physical geometry and informational geometry are identical. [2, 3, 4, 5]

## 1. From Thermodynamics: Causal Field Dynamics [6, 7] 

* The Traditional View: Entropy is a secondary, macroscopic description of heat loss and energy degradation.
* Obidi's Realignment: ToE elevates entropy to an active, continuous, and dynamic field. Temperature gradients and heat transfers are not byproducts; they are manifestations of the Obidi Action optimizing local entropic cost and flow. [1, 8, 9, 10, 11] 
* Physical Emergence: Gravity is reframed as "entropic pressure." Matter naturally moves toward massive objects because it is following gradients in the entropic field to maximize overall equilibrium. [1, 5, 9] 

## 2. From Statistical Mechanics: The Microstate Manifold

* The Traditional View: Boltzmann entropy counts the possible hidden microstates behind an observed macrostate.
* Obidi's Realignment: Obidi replaces the standard spacetime continuum with a differentiable entropic manifold. The points on this manifold are not empty geometric coordinates; they are primitive microstate configurations. Spacetime, curvature, and the universe's metric are merely macroscopic illusions arising from the deeper underlying relations of these microstates. [2, 12, 13, 14] 

## 3. From Information Theory: Ontological Information Geometry

* The Traditional View: Shannon entropy is epistemic—a measure of an observer's missing information or uncertainty about a data stream.
* Obidi's Realignment: Obidi makes information theory ontological (a physical reality, not just an observer's perspective). Using mathematical tools from information geometry (such as the Fisher-Rao metric and Amari-Čencov $\alpha$-connection), ToE establishes that the structural rules of data distinguishability dictate the literal laws of physical force and quantum mechanics. [2, 3, 4, 9, 15] 

------------------------------
## Key Theoretical Breakthroughs Derived from the Synthesis

* The Speed of Light ($c$) as an Entropic Limit: In ToE, $c$ is no longer an arbitrary geometric constant. It represents the maximum universal rate at which the entropic field can reconfigure itself to transmit data and rearrange energy. [1, 3, 5] 
* The "No-Rush" Theorem: Building directly on informational constraints, this theorem asserts that physical state changes require a non-zero, finite interval to build up entropic curvature. This explains causality and dictates that quantum entanglement or wave-function collapse cannot occur instantaneously. [1, 5, 16] 
* The Master Entropic Equation (MEE): Functioning as the entropic analogue to Einstein's Field Equations, the MEE uses a variational principle to mathematically map how the flow of the entropic field dictates the motion of energy and the curvature of reality. [5, 17]
