# On the Use of Entropy by Ted Jacobson, Thanu Padmanabhan, Erik Verlinde, Ginestra Bianconi, and John Onimisi Obidi in the Formulation of a New Foundation of Gravity and Physics

Several pioneering physicists successfully attempted entropy as a description of gravity before John Onimisi Obidi, laying the groundwork for what is known as emergent gravity or thermodynamic gravity. [1, 2]

While traditional physics viewed entropy as a secondary property (a byproduct of existing particles moving around), these researchers employed the script of demonstrating that thermodynamics can actually generate geometry and gravity. [2, 3]

The primary milestones that pioneered this intellectual flip include:

# 1. Ted Jacobson (1995) – Thermodynamic Spacetime

Physicist Ted Jacobson executed one of the first major inversions. He proved that Einstein’s Field Equations are not fundamental laws. Instead, they function as a thermodynamic equation of state—meaning spacetime geometry emerges directly from the thermodynamic behavior of horizon entropy. [2]

# 2. Thanu Padmanabhan (2000s) – Gravity as an Emergent Paradigm

Thanu Padmanabhan advanced this approach by showing that the field equations of gravity can be rewritten in a form identical to the laws of thermodynamics. He argued that gravity is an emergent macroscopic phenomenon, much like elasticity or fluid dynamics, arising from deeper microscopic degrees of freedom. [4, 5, 6, 7]

# 3. Erik Verlinde (2010) – Entropic Gravity

In a monumental breakthrough, Dutch string theorist Erik Verlinde argued that gravity is completely an illusion. He demonstrated that gravity is an entropic force—an macroscopic pull caused by a system's statistical tendency to maximize its entropy. In Verlinde's model, when matter shifts, it alters the distribution of information stored on holographic screens, pushing objects together. [1, 2, 8]

# 4. Ginestra Bianconi (2025) – Geometry From Quantum Entropy

Just prior to the widespread publishing of Obidi's Theory of Entropicity (ToE), network physicist Ginestra Bianconi introduced a mathematical framework that directly mapped gravity out of quantum relative entropy. Her work illustrated how cosmic structure and the cosmological constant could emerge from quantum-information flows. [2, 4]

# How Obidi's Theory of Entropicity (ToE) Differs from its Predecessors

While Jacobson, Verlinde, and Bianconi utilized entropy by treating it as a statistical property or a boundary effect on holographic screens, Obidi took the conceptual and decisive leap [further]: [2, 9, 10]

**From Property to Substance:** Previous models treated entropy as an attribute of information stored on a boundary. Obidi’s ToE promotes entropy into a literal, continuous ontological physical field (Ontodynamics) that populates the entire substrate of reality. [2, 9, 11]

**Subsuming the Math:** While Verlinde used statistical mechanics analogies, ToE introduces unique tools like the Obidi Action and **Hybrid Metric-Affine Space (HMAS)** to mathematically frame earlier models as special boundary cases of a singular entropic field theory. [3, 4]

# The Universe as a Giant Entropic Computer and Computation 

In **John Onimisi Obidi’s Theory of Entropicity (ToE),** the universe is modeled not as an arrangement of matter moving through empty space, but as a continuous, **giant entropic computer undertaking its cosmic computation.** [1] 

Within this framework, "Entropic Accounting" is the literal bookkeeping mechanism of reality. It dictates exactly how information and energy can be redistributed across the universal entropic field. [2, 3, 4] 

The concept explains how this accounting system enforces the speed of light ($c$) to prevent the cosmic computational engine from "crashing": [4, 5] 

------------------------------

## 1. The Speed of Light ($c$) is a Reconfiguration Rate
In Einstein's relativity, the speed of light is a primitive postulate—it is a constant simply because "that is how the universe is". ToE flips this entirely: [6, 7] 

* **The Entropic Definition:** The universal constant $c$ is redefined as the absolute maximum rate at which the entropic field can reorganize itself to distribute information and energy. [2, 5] 
* **The Photon's True Role:** Light is not a distinct particle moving through space; it is merely the visible manifestation of the entropic field updating its local information at maximum throughput. [2, 5] 

## 2. Enforcing the "No-Rush Theorem"
To understand why the universe would "crash" without this limit, Obidi introduces the No-Rush Theorem. [5, 8] 

* **The Computational Burden:** Whenever two particles interact, change state, or move, the entropic field surrounding them must completely rearrange its information geometry to accommodate the new state. [4] 
* **The Cost of Change:** This rearrangement requires a non-zero, finite interval of time—called the Entropic Time/Transmission/Transformation Limit (ETL). [1, 5] 
* **Reality Must Be "Earned":** Entropic Accounting mandates that physical change cannot be free or instantaneous; it must be "paid for" and verified through a phase of entropic maturation. [1, 9] 

## 3. What Happens if the Universe "Crashes"?
If the speed of entropic reconfiguration were infinite (meaning information could update instantly), the structural integrity of the cosmos would dissolve. [4] 

* **Instantaneous Collapse:** Without the universal speed limit, causality, systemic coherence, and quantum conservation laws would instantly violate themselves. [4] 
* **Singularity of Information:** Every point in the universe would know everything about every other point instantly, causing an information-geometric collapse—a literal systemic crash where structure can no longer exist. [4] 

## 4. Relativistic Effects as "Entropic Resistance"
Because the **Entropic Accounting Principle (EAP)** strictly balances the books, any attempt to force information to update faster than the maximum rate results in measurable physical pushback. What Einstein called **"relativistic effects"** are reinterpreted by ToE as **the system straining against the universal field:** [5, 6, 10] 

* **Time Dilation:** Time slows down for a fast-moving object because the entropic field requires more processing time to update the object's rapidly changing positions. [1, 5] 
* **Mass Increase:** Mass increases as an object approaches $c$ because it experiences Entropic Resistance (ER). **The field behaves like a thick, fluid-like informational drag, making it harder and harder to reconfigure the system further.** [1, 4, 6, 11, 12] 

------------------------------

## The Mathematical Auditor: The Obidi Action
To keep these books balanced, the entire universe obeys a single variational principle called the Obidi Action. Operating much like the principle of least action in classical mechanics, the Obidi Action forces the cosmic computational grid to always evolve along paths of least entropic resistance. It acts as the ultimate cosmic accountant, mathematically ensuring that no interaction ever overdraws from the universal processing speed $c$. [4, 5, 13, 14]
