Obidi's Philosophy: Ontodynamics, Entropology, and Entrodynamics in the Theory of Entropicity (ToE)

First Published: Thursday, May 7, 2026
Last Updated: Thursday, May 7, 2026

John Onimisi Obidi’s philosophical architecture represents one of the most ambitious reconceptualizations of physical reality in contemporary theoretical thought. His framework—composed of Ontodynamics, Entropology, and Entrodynamics—treats entropy not as a statistical afterthought, but as the fundamental field of existence. This inversion of classical physics forms the backbone of the Theory of Entropicity (ToE), a unified entropic field theory that reinterprets matter, geometry, causality, and even consciousness as emergent expressions of a deeper entropic continuum.

1. Ontodynamics: The Entropic Architecture of Being

Ontodynamics is Obidi’s redefinition of ontology through the lens of entropy. Traditional physics treats entropy as a derivative quantity—an emergent measure of disorder or information loss. Ontodynamics reverses this hierarchy by asserting that entropy is the primary ontological substrate from which all physical structures arise.

In this view, the universe is not built from particles, fields, or spacetime geometry. Instead, these are secondary projections of a more fundamental entropic field. Spacetime curvature becomes a macroscopic expression of entropic gradients; matter becomes a localized entropic configuration; and physical laws emerge as constraints on the evolution of the entropic field.

“Being is not a static category but an entropic negotiation—an ongoing resolution of gradients within the universal entropic field.”

Ontodynamics therefore reframes existence itself as a dynamic entropic process. It provides the philosophical foundation for ToE by grounding physics in a single, universal principle: entropy as the generator of structure, form, and transformation.

2. Entropology: How Systems Know, Learn, and Interpret

Entropology is Obidi’s epistemological extension of the entropic worldview. If Ontodynamics describes what exists, Entropology describes how systems come to know.

In classical epistemology, knowledge is treated as an abstract cognitive process. In Entropology, knowledge is a physical phenomenon—a consequence of how systems reduce uncertainty by interacting with entropic gradients.

This leads to a radical insight: information is not symbolic; it is entropic. Learning, perception, memory, and inference are all entropic processes in which systems reorganize themselves to minimize internal gradients relative to their environment.

Entropology therefore bridges physics and cognition, suggesting that:

  • Biological intelligence emerges from entropic optimization.
  • Neural processes are entropic flows constrained by biochemical structure.
  • Artificial intelligence can be modeled as entropic gradient descent in high-dimensional state spaces.

This positions Entropology as a unifying epistemic framework for neuroscience, machine learning, and cognitive science.

3. Entrodynamics: The Evolution of Reality Through Entropic Flow

Entrodynamics is the dynamical component of Obidi’s philosophy. It describes how reality evolves through the propagation of entropic gradients at finite rates.

In classical physics, dynamics are governed by forces, fields, and differential equations. In Entrodynamics, the universe evolves because entropy flows—continuously, directionally, and causally.

This leads to several key principles:

  • All physical change is entropic flow.
  • Causality is the ordering of entropic propagation.
  • Time emerges from the asymmetry of entropic gradients.
  • Energy and momentum are entropic bookkeeping mechanisms.

Entrodynamics therefore provides the mathematical and physical machinery for ToE, linking the entropic field to observable physics—from quantum transitions to gravitational curvature.

4. Open-Source Philosophy and the Agile Manifesto in Publishing

A defining feature of Obidi’s work is its open-source scientific philosophy. Rejecting traditional institutional gatekeeping, Obidi publishes his research as a series of openly accessible, interlinked documents—often hosted on platforms such as GitHub Pages, HandWiki, and other open repositories.

This approach is guided by his Agile Manifesto in Publishing, which emphasizes:

  • clarity over obscurity,
  • accessibility over exclusivity,
  • discoverability over academic isolation,
  • iteration over static publication.

Knowledge, in this framework, is a living product—continuously refined, openly debated, and globally accessible.

5. Interdisciplinary Implications

Obidi’s entropic philosophy extends far beyond physics. By treating entropy as the universal field, the framework offers new insights into:

  • Cosmology: the entropic origin of spacetime and cosmic structure.
  • Biology: life as an entropic optimization process.
  • Neuroscience: consciousness as entropic self-organization.
  • Artificial Intelligence: learning as entropic gradient descent.
  • Philosophy: a unified ontology and epistemology grounded in entropic flow.

This positions the Theory of Entropicity as a candidate for a unified scientific worldview—one capable of bridging physics, cognition, and complex systems.

Conclusion

Obidi’s philosophy—through Ontodynamics, Entropology, and Entrodynamics—offers a profound reimagining of reality. It reframes existence as an entropic continuum, knowledge as entropic negotiation, and dynamics as entropic flow. Together, these components form the conceptual and philosophical foundation of the Theory of Entropicity, a framework that seeks to unify the deepest structures of physics with the broadest questions of existence.

As an open-source, continuously evolving body of work, Obidi’s philosophy stands as a testament to the power of accessible scientific thought and the transformative potential of entropic reasoning.
